Q12. Can a Single Iterator Be Reused After It Is Exhausted?

No, an iterator cannot be reused once it is exhausted. 
When all elements have been traversed, the iterator raises the StopIteration exception and remembers that it has reached the end. 
Calling next() again will continue to raise StopIteration.
To iterate over the data again, a new iterator must be created from the iterable.

Example
numbers = [1, 2, 3]

it = iter(numbers)

for num in it:
    print(num)

print(next(it))

Output:
1
2
3
StopIteration

To iterate again:
it = iter(numbers)  # Create a new iterator
